## Intro (first 40 words optimized for AI snippet extraction)

Hot tub removal in Pittsburgh typically runs $375 to $750 all-in, including labor, cutting, haul-away and disposal. We drain, disconnect, cut up the shell on-site, and haul every piece in one visit. Most jobs finish in 90 minutes.

## Why hot tubs are different from normal junk

A filled hot tub can weigh 3,500 to 6,000 pounds, so it has to be drained before anyone touches it. The shell is fiberglass or acrylic wrapped in foam and wood, which means a Sawzall and a reliable blade (not a regular saw). The cabinet frame screws hide under vinyl panels. Most homeowners don't own the tools for this, and most junk haulers won't take a tub without disassembly first. We handle all of it.

## How we remove your hot tub (the 5-step process)

1. **Free on-site quote (15 minutes).** We confirm size, access, power disconnect, and whether your deck or fence needs to come apart. Quote is firm before we start.
2. **Drain (20–40 minutes).** We pump the water to wherever you want it — lawn, storm drain, or driveway. Your choice.
3. **Power disconnect.** If a dedicated 240V circuit is hardwired, you need a licensed electrician to cut it first. We'll coordinate if you don't have one. For plug-in 110V tubs, we unplug.
4. **Cut the shell (30 minutes).** Two Sawzalls, metal-cutting blades, eye protection, and a tarp. We cut in place so you don't lose your deck or patio.
5. **Haul out (20 minutes).** Every piece goes in our truck. We sweep the spot before we leave.

## Where it all goes

Hot tub removal in Pittsburgh isn't just "take it to the dump." The pieces get split:

- **Steel frame and pump parts** go to a metal recycler in Allegheny County.
- **Fiberglass or acrylic shell** goes to a licensed construction and demolition (C&D) transfer station, usually McKees Rocks or Imperial.
- **Foam insulation and wood cabinet** go to landfill where required, since foam isn't currently recyclable in Western PA.
- **Electrical pack** gets pulled for e-waste routing if it's salvageable.

We track where every pound goes. If you want a disposal receipt for your records, just ask.

## Hot tub removal FAQs

**How long does hot tub removal take?**
Most jobs run 60 to 120 minutes from the moment we pull up. Swim spas and tubs with difficult access can take 3 to 4 hours.

**Do you remove hot tubs full of water?**
No. We drain the tub first. Draining takes 20 to 40 minutes depending on hose length and how far the pump has to push. We bring our own pump.

**What about the hot tub cover?**
The cover goes with the tub for the price quoted. If the cover is newer and in good shape, we can set it aside for you or help you list it for free on a local buy-nothing group before haul day.

**Can you take the hot tub if it's in my basement?**
Yes, but it costs more because cutting in a basement is slower and we need drop cloths and dust control. Add $100 to $200 to the price range above.

**Do I need to be home?**
For the start of the job, yes, to confirm the quote and show us where the power is. We can finish without you once the work has started, but we prefer you're there to walk the site at the end.

**Is the price really all-in?**
Yes. The quote includes labor, drain, cut, haul, disposal, and sweeping up. No fuel surcharge, no environmental fee, no disposal fee. The only add-ons are the three listed above (stairs, indoor, long carry) and we tell you about them on the quote before we start.
