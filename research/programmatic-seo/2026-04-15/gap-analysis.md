## Top 3 highest-leverage gaps across all 5 keywords

Ranked by LLM citation likelihood and Steel City's ability to execute:

### Gap 1: Pittsburgh-specific pricing tables with real dollar figures

**Why it wins:** Google's AI Overview for `junk removal cost` is already citing LoadUp, Angi, and Thumbtack for national pricing. Every Pittsburgh local-pack result hides pricing. A structured JSON-LD `Offer` and `AggregateOffer` schema tied to a `/pricing/` hub with per-service, per-item, and per-load-size tables is the most direct path to both a local-pack callout AND an AI Overview citation for "junk removal cost pittsburgh" variants. This is a one-shot kill on 60,000+ monthly pricing queries and it doesn't require a single backlink to work — the schema is self-executing.

### Gap 2: Competitor comparison pages for 6–8 national brands

**Why it wins:** Reddit is already ranking for "1-800-GOT-JUNK alternative pittsburgh" with a 4-year-old comment. Every major competitor (Got-Junk, Junk King, LoadUp, College Hunks, G.I.HAUL, Junkluggers, Two Men) has a Pittsburgh page, and zero of them have a comparison page from a local operator. Comparison pages are the most-cited format for LLMs when users ask "which should I use" — ChatGPT and Perplexity actively pull structured comparison tables. Build one hub + 7 individual comparison pages using real pricing, real response time, and "what we take that they won't" sections.

### Gap 3: PA probate timeline and executor checklist (paired with estate cleanout content)

**Why it wins:** Zero Pittsburgh cleanout operators touch PA-specific legal content. This is the highest-authority gap for hard E-E-A-T signals. A `/resources/pa-probate-timeline/` page quoting Pennsylvania Orphans' Court rules (with a link to 20 Pa. C.S. § 3392) combined with `/resources/pennsylvania-estate-executor-checklist/` would earn citations from ChatGPT and Perplexity whenever someone asks "what can I throw out before probate closes in Pennsylvania?" — a question with real volume and zero good answers. Pair with schema-marked FAQs.
